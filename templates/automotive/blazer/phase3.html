Phase 3: Final aesthetics

Sand-blast interior

The interior of the truck was more rusty than I would like. There were quite a few small holes developing on the driver side floor that if left alone, would have inlarged and made for an interesting drive. I opted to sand-blast the entire interior and primer it in preparation for the body shop. Let me tell you, sand-blasting takes forever! It's very slow going, but the results are quite impressive.

Front clip

I was initially planning on installing an 88-91 Blazer or Suburban front clip, but discovered online that fibreglass shops were making custom fendors/hoods that would convert the older body to a 99 style front-end! While it has been fairly costly (all told, probably about $2000, not including the paint) the end result is quite impressive.

The fiber glass fenders from Glassworks Unlimited in Huntington Beach CA. Rear fenders and the Front clip. I then bought a late 90's radiator support from a wrecking yard, to support it all. (The old frame is a different width than the newer frames, so you have to modify it a bit (weld some plate steel in, and drill a new hole for the body mount bolts)

Paint

On May 1st 2004 the blazer returned with a fresh coat of 1996 Corvette dark green paint! Ooh yeah!

Reassembly

Bed liner in cab

The entire cab interior (minus dash and doors) has been sprayed with linex.

Soft top

I purchased a soft top from Specialty Top Company in Colorado. I went for the Tan top, with tinted windows to compliment the dark green exterior. Putting the top on wasn't too bad. It was a little cold when I did it, so the top was a little tight and took a bit of work to stretch out and fit.

Vent windows

I've replaced all the seals on the vent windows and completely rebuilt them as they were getting rather worn and tired.

Bumpers

I was going to put a Reunel bumper on the truck but they don't make a rear bumper for the older blazers, and their front bumper weighs a *ton*. After doing some research, I decided to go the custom tube bumper route and came up with my own design. I'm not too bad at welding, but I don't have access to a tube bender (and can't quite justify the cost of buying one, just yet.) so I opted to have a local custom fab shop build my design. The shop is Wicked Fabrication and the two bumpers cost about $1200 to put together. I'm running a 9000lb winch up front, and the rear bumper hides a 6000lb winch as well.


Bushings

I've replaced the body mounts, as they were worn out, and will be replacing the spring/shock bushings as well.
